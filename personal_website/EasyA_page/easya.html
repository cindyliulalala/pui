EasyA

Based on our research, highly mobile users such as business travelers, remote workers, and international students, currently rely on communicating with people back home, carrying their paper records, or use their memory to inform new or existing providers of their medical history instead of actual data.
We aim to provide a way for our users to access their complete health data from anywhere. To do this, we created a digital personal medical journal for each user—a space to upload, edit, and store their medical history online for understanding their own health and sharing with their health care providers.

Contextual Inquiry

We conducted 12 contextual user-interviews using Direct Storytelling as the key method. The participants we interviewed represent a diversity of backgrounds, cultures, ages, and genders. These individuals had varied experiences in accessing their medical records.

Insights From Customer Journey Map & Affinity Diagram

Followed by an interpretation session, we generated a customer journey map and an affinity diagram. The four unexpected findings we gained on the current medical data accessing process are:‍‍‍‍

1

Highly mobile users need highly mobile solutions

2

Complex medical jargon inhibits people from understanding their own records

3

Current systems are outdated and difficult to use, will benefit from digitization

4

There is not enough understanding of insurance in the US among international students
